Chocolate Sheet Cake

Description

This recipe creates a delicious and moist Texas Sheet Cake. It starts with a mix of white sugar and flour, combined with a mixture of melted butter, water, and cocoa powder. After adding baking soda, buttermilk, eggs, and vanilla extract, the batter is poured into a greased jelly roll pan and baked until done. For the rich frosting, a combination of butter, cocoa powder, and milk is heated and mixed with confectioners' sugar and chopped pecans. Once the cake has cooled, the frosting is poured over the top, creating a sweet and nutty finish to this classic dessert.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups white sugar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup butter, melted
  • 1 cup water
  • 3 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ½ cup buttermilk
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup butter
  • 3 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 5 tablespoons milk
  • 1 (16 ounce) box confectioners' sugar
  • 1 cup chopped pecans

Steps

  1. Preheat an oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease an 11x17 jelly roll pan.
  2. Combine the white sugar and flour in a large bowl. In a small saucepan, melt 1 cup of butter and combine it with water and 3 tablespoons of cocoa powder. Stir the mixture over medium heat until the butter is melted and everything is well combined. Pour this melted butter mixture over the sugar and flour, then beat everything together well. Add the baking soda, buttermilk, eggs, and vanilla extract, and beat the batter until smooth. Finally, pour the batter into the prepared pan.
  3. Bake in the preheated oven for 20 to 25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean. Remove the cake from the oven and let it cool on a wire rack.
  4. In a small saucepan, combine the remaining 1/2 cup of butter, 3 tablespoons of cocoa powder, and milk. Stir over medium heat until the butter is melted and the mixture is well combined. Beat the powdered sugar into the butter mixture until the frosting is smooth. Stir in the pecans, then pour the frosting over the cooled cake.
